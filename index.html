them apart. Understandably I broke them. I never really grew up which led to
this hobby, turned career.

The above statement is pretty much how I work. I pull things apart to fully understand all aspects in order to grasp the bigger picture. Nowadays I actually try my hardest not to break things, honest.

Fueled by none other than my determination to succeed in my dream career, I learnt as much as I can through practice. While I may not be so inclined to use fancy words such as 'kerning' I've found out that a having a good eye, a splash of raw talent, seasoned with an ability to identify and solve problems, makes for clients with happy endings - You read that right.

For the last two years I have been working freelance to gain experience and I can honestly say I have come a long way since 2011. I've built countless websites however this is the first I have built completely from scratch. Things like these are my education.

It's all about me.

1996-2006

I actually decided I wanted to get into Graphic Design when I was 18. I had always been a bit of a whizz-kid, I had my own DOS based computer at eight years old and the first Adobe product I got my mits on was Photoshop CS. It all started there.

I suppose studying for a degree can go one of two ways; you get that depth in the subject that interests you or like me, you find out that the subject no longer interests you, at all. I wouldn't change the fact that I have a Business and Management Degree, I'm actually very thankful for it. I decided to keep on with my part-time job in retail after University, eventually going full time with my ambitions set to the upper echelons of management; it made sense.

After coming to terms that no-one in retail gets paid appropriately for their efforts, I planned my escape. I did little bits of graphic and web design and eventually landed my first big (retrospectively, tiny) client and that was it, if I handed my notice in any quicker I would have seriously hurt someone.

Fueled by none other than my determination to succeed in my dream career, I learnt as much as I can through practice. While I may not be so inclined to use fancy words such as 'kerning' I've found out that a having a good eye, a splash of raw talent, seasoned with ability to identify and solve problems, makes for clients with happy endings - You read that right.

Me and my work.

A local micro-brewery had been ressurected after over a one hundred year absence. They needed a complete brand identity including three beer emblems that was true to it's history but with a modern twist.

Jade set up her new mobile upmarket bridal and special occasion hairdressing company and needed a logo. Jade works wonders with bows and has made that a fundamental focus on her branding materials.

Tom decided to take his business, the Helicopter Aviation Academy in Blackpool to the next level; taking his brand identity with it. I gave his business a flying start, with a new logo and printed materials.

Front Line Fit, is a fitness company and they have been one of my biggest clients to date. This particular image shows a few pages of their 'beginners booklet' that I co-designed with Julie Shenton.

Buckhurst Plant Hire is one of the biggest plant hire companies in the UK. They felt it was time to update their site and allow them the use of a content management system so they can manage the website in-house.

Tower Forestry had used the same branding for over a decade. Not only did I give them a brand new look, but sorted them out with new printed material and put them on online with a lovely new website.

A local business who specialises in the buying and selling of commercial vehicles. They only wanted some more business cards printed but later chose to update their look while they're at it.

Becca is a Holistic Therapist who offers a plethora of medical and relaxation services - even baby massage classes! Becca came to me for a logo, business card, leaflet and poster design.

The PowerStation Gym in Mossley is truly one of a kind. They not only wanted to create a gym, but an ultra modern atmosphere that people love to be in. I had the pleasure of creating their website.
